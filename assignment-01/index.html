Top 5 Hanna-Barbera Cartoons List

In this assignment, we will list the top 5 Hanna-Barbera Cartoons of all time!

This list is my opinion only, not gathered from any surveys or copied from Top 100 cartoon lists.

  1. Tom & Jerry (1940)

    The oscar winning cartoon debuted in 1940 with its first short film "Puss Gets the Boot".

    The beloved cartoon has maintained its popularity due to their two love-hate characters, Tom Cat and Jerry Mouse.

    Their unique back and forth hijinks sparked an entire new genre of slap stick comedy replicated by many other cartoons.

    Tom & Jerry, in my opinion, is the best animated cartoon of all time.

  2. Scooby Doo, Where Are You! (1969)

    Although only produced by Hanna-Barbera, Scooby Doo has had continued success even after almost six decades.

    Scooby Doo introduced children to classic horror and monster tropes that are still being used and replicated to this day.

    Its two main characters, Shaggy and Scobby are considered one of the best cartoon duos that ever existed.

  3. The Flinstones (1960)

    Created by Hanna-Barbera, the Flinstones were the most successful and longest-running network animated TV series for three decades.

    What made it unique at the time was how it showed "modern" day problems in a stone age setting.

    Resembling the sitcoms of that era, the animation made it appealing to children but entertaining enough for parents to watch as well.

  4. The Jetsons (1962)

    Created by Hanna-Barbera, following the success of the Flinstones, the animated cartoon replicated the family dynamics and storylines, but in the future Space Age

    Although it did not receive the same acclaim that Flinstone received, only lasting one season, it regained popularity when new episodes aired in 1985.

    The 1990 movie, Jetsons: The Movie, was a big staple and recurring rewatch in my childhood.

  5. Top Cat (1961)

    Created by Hanna-Barbera, the series only lasted one season due to low ratings.

    Although it was never really successful in the U.S., it was incredibly popular in Mexico.

    The spanish version of the cartoon is still my favorite version to watch.

    Only airing on Sunday mornings in Mexico, the show was a favorite of mine growing up.
